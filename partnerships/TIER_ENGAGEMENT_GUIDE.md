# Tier Engagement Guide

**Purpose:** Tier-specific approach for engaging partners. Aligned with 4-tier licensing model from phyllux-framework.

**Last Updated:** January 25, 2026

---

## Tier 1: Pioneer (Mission-Critical, Exclusive)

![Tier Structure Visualization](visuals/partnerships/tier-structure-visualization.svg)

### Characteristics
- Exclusive or semi-exclusive licensing
- Mission-critical applications
- High-value partnerships
- Strategic collaboration

### Engagement Approach

**Initial Contact:**
- Send urgent brief (see partnerships/briefs/_URGENT_*.md)
- Emphasize mission alignment
- Highlight exclusive potential
- Focus on transformative impact

**Follow-Up:**
- Schedule technical discussion within 2 weeks
- Provide detailed technical validation data
- Discuss exclusive licensing terms
- Explore strategic collaboration opportunities

**Negotiation:**
- Upfront license fee: $5-20M
- Annual royalties: 2-5% of revenue
- Milestone payments: $2-10M per milestone
- Equity (optional): 1-5% equity stake

**Timeline:**
- Initial contact: Post-PPA (late January/early February 2026)
- Technical discussion: 2-4 weeks after initial contact
- Negotiation: 2-6 months
- Agreement: 6-12 months

### Target Partners
- Tier 1 BCI (Phyllux Mesh + BCIs)
- Tier 1 Satellite (Phyllux Wave + Satellites)

## Tier 2: Mission-Aligned (Non-Exclusive, Mission-Driven)

### Characteristics
- Non-exclusive licensing
- Mission-driven organizations
- Mission discount applied
- Collaborative partnerships

### Engagement Approach

**Initial Contact:**
- Send mission-aligned brief (see partnerships/briefs/_template_mission.md)
- Emphasize mission alignment
- Highlight social impact
- Focus on collaborative benefits

**Follow-Up:**
- Schedule mission alignment discussion within 3 weeks
- Discuss mission discount structure
- Explore collaboration opportunities
- Review shared goals

**Negotiation:**
- Upfront license fee: $1-5M (discounted)
- Annual royalties: 1-3% of revenue (discounted)
- Milestone payments: $0.5-2M per milestone
- Equity (optional): 0.5-2% equity stake

**Timeline:**
- Initial contact: Post-PPA (February-March 2026)
- Mission discussion: 3-6 weeks after initial contact
- Negotiation: 3-9 months
- Agreement: 9-18 months

### Target Partners
- Medtronic (Phyllux Mesh + Neuroprosthetics)
- Boston Scientific (Phyllux Mesh + Neuromodulation)
- Google (Phyllux Core + Energy Efficiency)
- Microsoft (Phyllux Core + Energy Efficiency)

## Tier 3: Commercial (Standard Licensing)

### Characteristics
- Standard commercial terms
- Broad market access
- Competitive pricing
- Non-exclusive (typically)

### Engagement Approach

**Initial Contact:**
- Send commercial brief (see partnerships/briefs/_template_commercial.md)
- Emphasize commercial value
- Highlight competitive advantages
- Focus on market opportunity

**Follow-Up:**
- Schedule technical validation discussion within 4 weeks
- Provide technical validation data
- Discuss standard licensing terms
- Review market opportunity

**Negotiation:**
- Upfront license fee: $2-10M
- Annual royalties: 2-4% of revenue
- Milestone payments: $1-5M per milestone
- Equity (optional): 0.5-1% equity stake

**Timeline:**
- Initial contact: Post-PPA (March-June 2026)
- Technical discussion: 4-8 weeks after initial contact
- Negotiation: 4-12 months
- Agreement: 12-24 months

### Target Partners
- Ericsson (Phyllux Wave + 5G/6G)
- Nokia (Phyllux Wave + 5G/6G)
- Samsung Networks (Phyllux Wave + 5G/6G)
- Amazon Kuiper (Phyllux Wave + Satellites)
- OneWeb (Phyllux Wave + Satellites)

## Tier 4: Research (Free Access)

### Characteristics
- Free access for research
- Open source compatible
- MIT license for code
- Attribution required

### Engagement Approach

**Initial Contact:**
- Send research brief (see partnerships/briefs/_template_research.md)
- Provide code access (MIT license)
- Offer research collaboration
- Support academic work

**Follow-Up:**
- Provide technical support
- Track research publications
- Monitor citations
- Explore future commercial opportunities

**Terms:**
- Free access (no licensing fees)
- MIT license for code
- CC BY-SA 4.0 for documentation
- Attribution required

**Timeline:**
- Initial contact: Ongoing
- Research collaboration: Ongoing
- Publication tracking: Ongoing
- Future commercial opportunities: As they arise

### Target Partners
- Academic research labs
- Open source projects
- Educational institutions
- Research organizations

## Engagement Workflow

### Phase 1: Preparation
1. Identify target partner
2. Select appropriate tier
3. Generate tier-specific brief
4. Research partner's mission/needs
5. Identify key contacts

### Phase 2: Initial Contact
1. Send brief via email
2. Include links to public repos
3. Set expectation for follow-up
4. Log in partnerships/pipeline/active_conversations.md

### Phase 3: Follow-Up
1. Follow up after appropriate timeframe (2-4 weeks)
2. Schedule discussion call
3. Prepare technical validation data
4. Review partner's needs

### Phase 4: Negotiation
1. Discuss licensing terms
2. Review technical validation
3. Explore collaboration opportunities
4. Negotiate agreement structure

### Phase 5: Agreement
1. Finalize licensing terms
2. Execute agreement
3. Log in partnerships/pipeline/closed_deals.md
4. Begin partnership execution
