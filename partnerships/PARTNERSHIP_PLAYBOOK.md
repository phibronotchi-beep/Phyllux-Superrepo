# Partnership Playbook

**Purpose:** Comprehensive guide to partnership development, from initial contact to closed deal. Covers strategy, tactics, and best practices.

**Last Updated:** January 25, 2026

---

## Partnership Development Process

![Partnership Funnel](visuals/partnerships/partnership-funnel.svg)

### Stage 1: Opportunity Identification

**Sources:**
- TOP_5_LANES.md (priority opportunities)
- PARTNER_TARGET_LIST.md (specific organizations)
- OPPORTUNITY_MATRIX.md (scored fusions)
- Market research and industry analysis

**Criteria:**
- Market size (TAM)
- Technology maturity
- Strategic alignment
- Partner fit

**Output:**
- Prioritized partner list
- Tier assignment
- Technology mapping
- Initial brief concept

---

### Stage 2: Brief Generation

**Process:**
1. Select tier template (see partnerships/briefs/)
2. Customize for partner and technology
3. Include technical validation data
4. Add mission alignment (if Tier 2)
5. Review for qualified language
6. Mark as "ready to send" or "needs review"

**Templates:**
- _template_pioneer.md (Tier 1)
- _template_mission.md (Tier 2)
- _template_commercial.md (Tier 3)
- _template_research.md (Tier 4)

**Automation:**
- Use analytics/brief_generator.py for initial draft
- Manual customization required
- Review for accuracy and tone

---

### Stage 3: Initial Contact

**Timing:**
- Post-PPA for Tiers 1-3 (late January/early February 2026)
- Ongoing for Tier 4 (research)

**Method:**
- Email with brief attachment
- Include links to public repos
- Set expectation for follow-up
- Professional, concise tone

**Logging:**
- Record in partnerships/pipeline/active_conversations.md
- Note tier, technology, status
- Set follow-up date

---

### Stage 4: Follow-Up

**Timing:**
- Tier 1: 2 weeks after initial contact
- Tier 2: 3 weeks after initial contact
- Tier 3: 4 weeks after initial contact
- Tier 4: As needed (research collaboration)

**Content:**
- Acknowledge receipt of brief
- Offer to schedule discussion
- Provide additional information if requested
- Maintain professional tone

**Logging:**
- Update partnerships/pipeline/active_conversations.md
- Note response status
- Update next action date

---

### Stage 5: Technical Discussion

**Preparation:**
- Review partner's mission/needs
- Prepare technical validation data
- Review licensing framework
- Prepare for questions

**Discussion Points:**
- Technical validation and feasibility
- Licensing terms and structure
- Mission alignment (if Tier 2)
- Timeline and next steps

**Follow-Up:**
- Send summary of discussion
- Provide requested information
- Schedule next steps
- Update pipeline status

---

### Stage 6: Negotiation

**Preparation:**
- Review REVENUE_MODEL.md for deal structure
- Understand partner's needs
- Prepare negotiation strategy
- Review ethical boundaries

**Negotiation Points:**
- Upfront license fee
- Annual royalties
- Milestone payments
- Equity (if applicable)
- Exclusivity (if Tier 1)
- Mission discount (if Tier 2)

**Documentation:**
- Track negotiation progress
- Document agreed terms
- Update pipeline status
- Prepare agreement draft

---

### Stage 7: Agreement Execution

**Finalization:**
- Review agreement terms
- Legal review (if needed)
- Finalize documentation
- Execute agreement

**Post-Agreement:**
- Log in partnerships/pipeline/closed_deals.md
- Begin partnership execution
- Schedule technical validation
- Plan milestone tracking

---

## Best Practices

### Communication

**Do:**
- ✅ Use qualified language ("may", "designed to", "simulations suggest")
- Be professional and concise
- Emphasize mission alignment (if Tier 2)
- Highlight competitive advantages
- Provide technical validation data

**Don't:**
- ❌ Make absolute performance claims
- ❌ Overpromise capabilities
- ❌ Rush negotiations
- ❌ Ignore partner's needs
- ❌ Violate ethical boundaries

---

### Brief Generation

**Do:**
- ✅ Use tier-specific templates
- ✅ Customize for partner and technology
- ✅ Include technical validation data
- ✅ Review for qualified language
- ✅ Include links to public repos

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use generic content
- ❌ Make absolute claims
- ❌ Skip technical validation
- ❌ Ignore mission alignment (if Tier 2)
- ❌ Forget to review

---

### Negotiation

**Do:**
- ✅ Understand partner's needs
- ✅ Be flexible on terms
- ✅ Maintain ethical boundaries
- ✅ Document everything
- ✅ Set realistic expectations

**Don't:**
- ❌ Rush negotiations
- ❌ Ignore partner's concerns
- ❌ Violate ethical boundaries
- ❌ Make unrealistic promises
- ❌ Skip documentation

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Partner Requests Exclusivity (Tier 1)

**Response:**
- Exclusivity is negotiable for Tier 1 partnerships
- Discuss scope of exclusivity (technology, market, geography)
- Consider exclusivity fee
- Review ethical boundaries

**Action:**
- Negotiate exclusivity terms
- Document in agreement
- Update pipeline status

---

### Scenario 2: Partner Requests Mission Discount (Tier 2)

**Response:**
- Mission discount is available for Tier 2 partnerships
- Review mission alignment
- Apply standard mission discount (20-40% reduction)
- Document in agreement

**Action:**
- Apply mission discount
- Document in agreement
- Update pipeline status

---

### Scenario 3: Partner Requests Research Access (Tier 4)

**Response:**
- Research access is free (Tier 4)
- Provide code access (MIT license)
- Offer research collaboration
- Track publications and citations

**Action:**
- Provide code access
- Log research collaboration
- Track publications
- Monitor for future commercial opportunities

---

### Scenario 4: Partner Requests Technical Validation

**Response:**
- Provide available technical validation data
- Offer to conduct additional validation
- Discuss validation partnership
- Review validation timeline

**Action:**
- Provide validation data
- Schedule validation discussion
- Plan validation partnership
- Update pipeline status

---

## Pipeline Management

### Active Conversations

**Track:**
- Partner name
- Tier
- Technology
- Status
- Last contact
- Next action
- Urgency

**Update:**
- Weekly review
- Update status regularly
- Set follow-up dates
- Note important developments

---

### Proposals Sent

**Track:**
- Partner name
- Brief sent date
- Technology
- Tier
- Response status
- Follow-up date

**Update:**
- Log when brief sent
- Track response status
- Schedule follow-ups
- Note outcomes

---

### Closed Deals

**Track:**
- Partner name
- Deal date
- Technology
- Tier
- Deal value
- Terms summary

**Update:**
- Log when deal closed
- Document terms
- Track revenue
- Monitor execution
